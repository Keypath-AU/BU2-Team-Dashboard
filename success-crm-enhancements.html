Enrolment Blocker Identification & CRM Visibility
High
Identify and surface external enrolment blockers — such as LANTITE and AIM — within the CRM to enable proactive student intervention.
▼ Click for details
Various external requirements can block a student from enrolling into their next unit — including LANTITE (English and maths proficiency assessments), Academic Integrity Modules (AIM), and other partner-specific compliance obligations. Currently there is no consistent visibility of these blockers within the CRM. Reports tracking student completion status are received from partner institutions, but there is no structured view of when these reports arrive, what they contain, or how advisers should act on them. This leaves the team unable to identify and contact at-risk students in time to prevent a loss of enrolment. This initiative will map all known enrolment blockers across each partner, assess current reporting pathways (what reports exist, where they originate, what dates they are received), and identify gaps in CRM visibility. The outcome will be the creation of one or more CRM fields or views — fed by reports requested from partner institutions — that give advisers clear, actionable visibility of each student's compliance status against these requirements. The goal is to reduce student attrition caused by non-completion of mandatory modules or assessments, enabling the team to proactively support at-risk students before a blocker prevents their next enrolment.

CRM Call Classification & Intent to Enrol Enhancement
High
Automate call subject assignment using existing CRM data, and make Intent to Enrol a mandatory, dynamically editable field.
▼ Click for details
SSAs are currently required to manually assign a call type/subject at the end of every logged call. This introduces inconsistency across teams and relies entirely on adviser compliance at the point of wrap-up. The data needed to determine the majority of call types — specifically Enrolment Status and Next Term Status — already exists in the CRM. Automating this assignment would reduce manual effort, improve data consistency, and free advisers to focus on higher-value tasks. Separately, the Intent to Enrol field is not mandatory and has no mechanism for post-call updates, limiting its reliability for forecasting and intervention planning. This initiative has two components: 1. Automated Call Subject Assignment The CRM will automatically assign the student position subject at wrap-up by reading existing enrolment data (Enrolment Status and Next Term Status) already available in the standard enrolment view, covering the five primary student position categories. Advisers will no longer manually select these. A secondary, optional tier is retained for calls that cannot be auto-classified — topic-based subjects such as T1s, Placement, Payment, and Risk. Advisers may assign one of these in addition to the auto-assigned subject where the call topic warrants it. 2. Mandatory Intent to Enrol on Call Wrap-Up For every call where a student was spoken to, the call cannot be closed until an Intent to Enrol value has been selected — removing the discretionary nature of the field and ensuring consistent data capture. The field must remain dynamically editable outside of the call workflow, allowing advisers and team leaders to update it at any time (e.g. if a student calls back the following day to confirm their enrolment decision).
Effort: Medium
